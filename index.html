PhD - University of East Anglia (2016-2020)

Title: Revealing DNA's Secret Structures: Developing an i-Motif Specific Fluorescent Probe for Cellular Imaging

Supervisors: Dr. Zoë A. E. Waller & Dr. John Fielden

i-Motif DNA has been shown to form in the promoter regions of many genes relating to various diseases. There currently exists no i-motif specific small molecule probe to help discover its biological significance, this project focusses on tackling exactly that; to develop a ruthenium-based i-motif specific probe.

Keywords: ruthenium-based fluorophores; transition metal polypyridyls; DNA secondary structures; DNA-ligand binding

MChem - University of East Anglia (2011-2015)

Title: Enhancing the Optical and Electropolymerisation Properties of Polyoxometalate-based Nonlinear Optical Chromophores

Supervisor: Dr. John Fielden

This research focussed around the synthesis of novel polyoxometalate-based chromophores and their subsequent spectroscopic characterisation and electropolymerisation. This yielded the discovery of a POM-based complex possessing the highest βzzz,0 known for such a material, breaching Kuzyk's apparent limit.

Keywords: polyoxometalate chemistry; nonlinear optics; spectroelectrochemistry; electropolymerisation
